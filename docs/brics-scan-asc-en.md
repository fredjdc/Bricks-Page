# Bricks Scan — App Store ASO Strategy

Last updated: April 28, 2026

## Goal

Increase qualified installs for Bricks Scan by improving:

- App Store search ranking for scanner, OCR, and document organization terms.
- Product page conversion from impression to install.
- Review volume after users complete clear value moments.

Bricks Scan should not read like a generic scanner. It should own a narrower promise:

Scan, organize, search, sign, and share real estate documents on Apple devices without uploading files to a cloud service.

## Positioning

Lead with the practical job:

Scan and find documents fast.

Then explain the difference:

Bricks Scan uses OCR, automatic naming, on-device organization, and optional iCloud Sync while keeping documents local by default.

Avoid broad claims like "AI-powered productivity" or "all-in-one document management." They are less searchable and less credible.

## Keyword Direction

Primary terms to defend:

- document scanner
- PDF scanner
- OCR scanner
- scanner for iPhone
- searchable scanner

Secondary terms to test:

- contract scanner
- real estate scanner
- scan to PDF
- offline scanner
- private scanner

## Product Page Direction

Recommended screenshot order:

1. Scan and export a clean PDF.
2. Search inside document text with OCR.
3. Automatic naming and organization.
4. Markup, notes, and signing on device.
5. Privacy and optional iCloud Sync across Apple devices.

Recommended caption direction:

- Scan to a clean PDF fast.
- Search the text inside every document.
- Keep contracts named and organized.
- Sign, review, and share without leaving the app.
- Private on device. Sync only when you choose.

## Conversion Notes

- Ask for reviews after a completed scan, successful text search, shared PDF, or completed signature workflow.
- Keep pricing and free-trial wording exact. Do not imply unlimited free usage if premium limits exist.
- Match the website promise: private, local-first, native on Apple, no account required.

## Website Alignment

- The website now uses campaign-tagged App Store links so installs can be attributed by page and section.
- App Store metadata should stay aligned with the website headline: scan, organize, search, sign, and share.
